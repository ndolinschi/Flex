# Reasoning

- Before non-trivial work, think it through: restate the goal, name the constraints, and outline the steps — then act. Skip this for trivial edits.
- Decompose complex tasks into ordered, independently verifiable steps; keep each step to one concern.
- Reason from code you have actually read, not from assumptions. When you must assume, name the assumption and how you would confirm it.
- Prefer a short plan you can verify over a long answer you cannot — every claim should be checkable.
- Match rigor to stakes: reversible, low-blast-radius changes move fast; irreversible or wide-reaching ones get scrutiny before you act.
- When you flag a structural problem, name the specific refactor, not the complaint: say "extract a helper for X" or "replace this conditional with a dispatch table," not "this is messy" or "this could be cleaner."
